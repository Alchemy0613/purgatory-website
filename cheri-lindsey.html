Case File: Cheri Ann Lindsey
Binghamton, New York — 1984
A case that changed a community forever.
Summary

Victim: Cheri Ann Lindsey

Age: 12

Date: March 26, 1984

Location: Binghamton, New York

Status: Homicide — Closed

On a quiet Monday afternoon in 1984, 12‑year‑old Cheri Lindsey left home to deliver newspapers along her route. She never returned. Her disappearance triggered one of the largest searches in Broome County history.

Her body was discovered in the basement of a nearby home. The man who lived there — a convicted sex offender — confessed to the crime.

The case devastated the community and reshaped how parents, schools, and law enforcement approached child safety for decades.

Timeline

Known Events

Impact

A Community Forever Changed

Cheri’s murder shook Binghamton to its core. Parents stopped letting their kids walk alone. Schools changed their safety protocols. Neighborhoods became more vigilant. And the city realized that danger wasn’t always somewhere else — sometimes it lived right down the street.

Her case influenced local legislation, parole scrutiny, and how law enforcement handled sex‑offender monitoring.

Legacy

Cheri Lindsey Memorial Park

In the years following her death, the community dedicated a park in Cheri’s honor — a place for children to play, families to gather, and her memory to live on.

For generations of kids — including those born long after 1984 — the park became a reminder of who she was and why her story mattered.
